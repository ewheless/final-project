If you are like me, you follow at least three (29) dogs (plus a cockateil, an alpaca, and a squirrel) on Instagram (as well as several plant stores). Without the space (or money) to care for their own puppers, New Yorkers are fans of their Insta-pets. Using the NYC Dog License data, this article will explore the breeds living in your neighborhood.

Each doggo owner in NYC is required by law to register and license their dog. According to the data, there are 25,670 dogs registered in NYC. The zip codes with the most dogs are in the Upper West Side, Brooklyn Heights/DUMBO, and Park Slope.

NYC is crowded, so smaller breeds are popular. The top five listed breeds are the Yorkshire Terrier, Shih Tzu, Chihuahua, and the Labrador Retriever. The top listed breed was "Unknown" but it isn't not clear if this was laziness on the part of the dog owner, a way of protecting their privacy, or accounting for the dog being a mixed breed/ mutt.
